Heads

 

The 1996 heads are different then all the years before it. The top end and the intake ports are unchanged but the exhaust ports are larger having a much wider oval shape to them. 

Intake port

Exhaust Port

The combustion chambers are tighter and have a lower volume as well as a clover-shaped surface. The compression was bumped up to about 9.5:1. Oddly enough, even with all these major changes, the engine only gained 5 h.p. Many believe that the engine is detuned, I totally agree. 

A look at both heads

A closer look at the combustion chambers

A clear look at the valve pocket

I have found a problem with my 96 heads that I believe could be a common problem or a factory mistake. The exhaust valve guides toward the center of the head (Cyls 3 and 4) were in the wrong spot, they are much lower into the exhaust ports then they should be, you can see them sticking out in one cyl and not in the other, this causes the valve guide seal to come loose and potentially the engine might smoke.
